Poojas or Rituals of the Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath Temple follows ancient Vedic traditions in its daily rituals. The temple opens only for six months from April/May to October/November due to heavy snowfall during winter. The daily worship schedule includes:

  • Morning Pooja (Abhishek and Maha Abhishek):
    • Starts around 4:00 AM. Priests perform the sacred Abhishek to the Shivlinga using water, milk, honey, ghee, and other offerings.

  • Rudra Abhishek:
    • A powerful form of Lord Shiva worship performed by chanting Vedic mantras with continuous pouring of holy water and milk.

  • Shodashopachara Pooja:
    • A 16-step ritual that includes offerings of flowers, incense, light, and food.

  • Evening Aarti:
    • Starts around 6:00 PM. Devotees gather to witness the grand Aarti, performed with lamps, bells, and chants.

  • Special Poojas:
    • Devotees can book specific poojas such as Laghu Rudra, Ekadasa Rudra, and Maha Rudra through the official portal.

Festivals Celebrated at Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath Temple celebrates various festivals that attract thousands of devotees from across the country:

  • Opening Day: Celebrated in April/May with great fervor. The idol of Lord Shiva is brought back from Ukhimath to Kedarnath amidst chanting and music.
  • Akshaya Tritiya: Considered highly auspicious for beginning the yatra season. Devotees offer special prayers.
  • Maha Shivaratri: One of the biggest festivals when devotees fast and perform night-long worship to honor Lord Shiva.
  • Raksha Bandhan: Special prayers and rakhi tying rituals are performed at the temple by priests on behalf of devotees.
