Poojas or Rituals of the Temple

The Kanchi Kamakshi Amman Temple follows a rich and traditional set of rituals and poojas that reflect deep reverence for Goddess Kamakshi, an incarnation of Parvati and the presiding deity of Kanchipuram. Here's an overview of the daily, weekly, and special rituals performed at the temple:

  • Suprabhata Seva: 5:30 AM
    • Temple doors open with the chanting of Suprabhatam to awaken the goddess.

  • Morning Pooja (Kalasanthi Pooja): 6:00 AM to 7:30 AM
    • Includes Abhishekam (on special days), Alankaram, Naivedyam, and Deepa Aradhana..

  • Uchikala Pooja (Midday Pooja): 12:00 PM to 12:30 PM
    • Midday offering with naivedyam, chanting, and lamp worship.

  • Sayaraksha Pooja (Evening Pooja): 6:00 PM to 6:30 PM
    • Evening worship with Deepa Aradhana, flower offerings, and mantras.

  • Ardhajama Pooja (Night Ritual): 8:30 PM to 9:00 PM
    • Final pooja where the goddess is symbolically put to rest with milk, fruits, and lullabies.

  • Sahasranama Archana :

    Festivals

    These are the important festivals celebrated at the Kanchi Kamakshi Amman Temple, focusing on key events throughout the Hindu calendar.These festivals are deeply rooted in Shakta traditions, especially honoring Goddess Kamakshi as the universal mother.

    • Day 1:Shailaputri: Kamakshi is worshipped as the daughter of the Himalayas, symbolizing strength and purity.
    • Day 2:Brahmacharini: Honored as the goddess of penance and devotion, dressed in yellow.
    • Day 3:Chandraghanta: Worshipped in a warrior form with a crescent moon, blessing devotees with courage.
    • Day 4:Kushmanda: Celebrated as the source of cosmic energy and universal creation.
    • Day 5:Skandamata: Revered as the loving mother of Lord Muruga, symbol of motherly grace.
    • Day 6:Katyayani: Fierce form of the goddess, destroyer of evil and protector of dharma.
    • Day 7:Kalaratri: Worshipped as the dark goddess who removes fear and ignorance.
    • Day 8:Mahagauri: Embodies peace, purity, and compassion, adorned in white.
    • Day 9:Siddhidatri: Bestower of spiritual powers and wisdom, worshipped through Sri Chakra pooja.
