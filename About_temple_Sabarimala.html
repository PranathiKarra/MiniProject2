SABARIMALA-SREE-DHARMA-SASTHA-TEMPLE

About Sabarimala Sree Dharma Sastha Temple

The Sabarimala Temple, located in the Periyar Tiger Reserve in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is one of the most famous and visited Hindu pilgrimage centers in India. Dedicated to Lord Ayyappa, the temple is situated atop a hill at an altitude of about 3000 feet and is surrounded by dense forests and mountains. Millions of devotees undertake the pilgrimage every year, especially during the Mandala and Makaravilakku seasons, following 41 days of strict vrata (austerity).

According to legend, Lord Ayyappa, the son of Lord Shiva and Mohini (an incarnation of Vishnu), meditated at Sabarimala after fulfilling his divine mission of slaying the demoness Mahishi. He is believed to have chosen this forested hilltop to attain samadhi (spiritual union). The temple symbolizes harmony and devotion, welcoming people of all castes and creeds, although traditionally, women between ages 10 and 50 have not been permitted entry.

Architecture of the Temple

Important aspects of the architecture of the Sabarimala Temple:

  1. The temple follows Kerala’s traditional architectural style, with a sanctum (Sreekovil) housing the idol of Lord Ayyappa in a yogic posture.
  2. The 18 sacred steps (Pathinettam Padi) leading to the sanctum are spiritually significant and can only be climbed by devotees who have completed the 41-day vrata.
  3. The temple complex also includes shrines for Ganapathi, Maligapurathu Amma, and other deities.
  4. The temple is constructed using stone and copper plating, and is adorned with gabled roofs and wooden carvings.

Facts about Sabarimala Temple

Sabarimala is steeped in mythology, devotion, and tradition. Here are some unique facts:

  1. Associated with Lord Ayyappa
    • The temple honors Lord Ayyappa, a celibate deity who embodies dharma, devotion, and renunciation.

  2. Mandala Vratham
    • Devotees undergo a 41-day spiritual observance involving celibacy, vegetarianism, and daily prayers before visiting the temple.

  3. 18 Holy Steps
    • The 18 steps represent various spiritual significances, including senses, gunas, and elements. Devotees revere these steps as sacred.

  4. Multifaith Symbolism
    • Vavar Mosque near the temple honors Vavar, a Muslim devotee of Ayyappa, symbolizing religious harmony.

  5. Pilgrimage Tradition
    • Most devotees begin their journey from Erumely and pass through dense forests, reflecting Ayyappa’s own journey and spiritual quest.

About Lord Ayyappa

    Lord Ayyappa, also known as Dharma Sastha, is a powerful Hindu deity symbolizing righteousness, celibacy, and detachment from materialism. He is the divine offspring of Lord Shiva and Mohini (the female incarnation of Lord Vishnu). Ayyappa is regarded as a protector of dharma and the destroyer of evil.

    After defeating the demoness Mahishi, Ayyappa chose the forested hill of Sabarimala as his place of meditation and eternal abode. The temple was built in his honor, and the pilgrimage to Sabarimala came to symbolize the victory of good over evil and self-discipline over desires.

    Ayyappa is depicted as a yogi seated in a meditative posture (Yogapatta), signifying his commitment to celibacy and spiritual wisdom. His devotees refer to each other as “Swamiye Saranam Ayyappa,” expressing total surrender to the Lord.

    The deity’s form represents a blend of Shaiva and Vaishnava traditions, and his temple is a confluence of Vedic, Tantric, and tribal customs. The pilgrimage is not just a journey of distance, but of spiritual transformation.

    The Sabarimala Temple, surrounded by forests and hills, continues to be a sacred sanctuary of devotion, meditation, and divine energy in the heart of Kerala.
