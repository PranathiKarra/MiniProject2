Tips and Rules for visiting the Temple

  1. Don’t wear revealing clothes that expose too much skin. Wear clothes that cover your legs and arms completely. Advisable for women to wear shawls. The temple authorities are very strict about the dress code so do make sure you are conservatively dressed.

  2. Wear traditional Indian clothing like Sarees or Half-Sarees for women and Dhoti or Pancha for men.

  3. Leave your footwear and socks in your car or bus. You can’t wear it in the temple premises

  4. Mobile phones, cameras, electronic items, sprays of any kind, hand bags, backpacks wrist watches, calculators etc. are not allowed. Remember to leave them behind. If you still happen to have them with you, you will be forced to leave them behind in a locker near the entrance for a nominal fee.

  5. Foreigners are allowed to visit the temple complex and admire the architecture and surroundings.

  6. Foreigners will have to pay Rs.50/- to enter the temple premises

  7. General Darshan is free of cost for Indian citizens.Special Darshan tickets at Trimbakeshwar cost ₹100 for quick access to the Jyotirlinga, ₹50 for basic line access, and are free for children below 10 years.

  8. A prasadam packet (usually a laddu) is included with the special darshan ticket.Additional prasadam can be purchased at designated counters near the exit.

  9. Mondays, Maha Shivaratri, Shravan month, and Karthik Purnima are particularly auspicious and attract large crowds.
