Best Season to Visit Sabarimala

November to January is the Best Season to Visit Sabarimala

Sabarimala is open to devotees during specific periods of the year, mainly from mid-November to mid-January, known as the Mandala and Makaravilakku seasons. This is considered the most auspicious time to visit, as millions of devotees undertake the pilgrimage during these months. The weather is also relatively cooler, making it suitable for the rigorous trek through the forested hills of Pathanamthitta district in Kerala.

Ways to Reach Sabarimala

By Air

The nearest airport is Trivandrum International Airport (174 km) and Cochin International Airport (160 km). From the airport, you can hire taxis or board buses to reach Pampa, the base station for the trek to Sabarimala.

By Rail

The nearest major railway stations are Chengannur (93 km), Kottayam (115 km), and Thiruvalla (99 km). From there, regular buses and cabs are available to Pampa. Pilgrims must continue the remaining 5 km journey by foot through the sacred forest path.

Book Train Tickets to Kerala

By Road

Sabarimala is well connected by road up to Pampa, the final motorable point. Regular bus services from KSRTC (Kerala State Road Transport Corporation) operate from cities like Thiruvananthapuram, Ernakulam, Kottayam, and Pathanamthitta. Private taxis and jeeps are also available.
