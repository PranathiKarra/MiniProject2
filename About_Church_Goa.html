BASILICA OF BOM JESUS CHURCH

About Basilica of Bom Jesus Church

The Basilica of Bom Jesus, located in Old Goa, is one of the most revered and historically significant churches in India. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, a Spanish missionary whose body is said to have miraculous powers. Built in the Baroque architectural style, the church is over 400 years old and stands as a testament to Goa's colonial past and rich Christian heritage.

Constructed in 1605, the name “Bom Jesus” means “Good Jesus” in Portuguese. This sacred structure attracts pilgrims from all over the world, especially during the decennial exposition when the saint’s relics are displayed. The church, with its elegant façade and intricately carved interiors, exudes spiritual serenity and artistic brilliance.

Architecture of the Church

Notable architectural features of the Basilica:

  1. Baroque style with Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian elements, made from black granite and laterite stone.
  2. The façade is simple yet grand, with Latin inscriptions and symbolic carvings.
  3. The main altar is richly gilded and features a statue of Infant Jesus and St. Ignatius Loyola.
  4. Inside, the marble mosaic flooring is embedded with precious stones and features a grand gilded woodwork ceiling.

Facts about the Basilica

The Basilica holds both religious and historical significance:

  1. Relics of St. Francis Xavier
    • His body is kept in a silver casket and is said to be incorrupt, making the church a major pilgrimage site.

  2. Decennial Exposition
    • Every ten years, his relics are brought out for public viewing, drawing lakhs of devotees and tourists.

  3. UNESCO World Heritage
    • Part of the Churches and Convents of Goa recognized by UNESCO for their cultural and architectural value.

  4. Jesuit Influence
    • The church is maintained by the Jesuit community and reflects their missionary zeal and legacy in Goa.

  5. Art and Frescoes
    • The interiors house paintings depicting the life of St. Francis Xavier and biblical scenes.

About St. Francis Xavier

    St. Francis Xavier was a Spanish missionary and one of the founding members of the Society of Jesus (Jesuits). He arrived in Goa in 1542 and spent years spreading Christianity across India, Sri Lanka, Malaysia, and Japan. His dedication, compassion, and miracles earned him sainthood and immense reverence.

    After his death in 1552 in China, his body was brought to Goa and found to be remarkably preserved. It was interred in the Basilica of Bom Jesus, which became his final resting place. His missionary efforts greatly influenced the religious fabric of Goa and Asia.

    His feast day is celebrated on December 3rd each year, accompanied by grand processions, prayers, and a large influx of pilgrims.

Church Services and Rituals

The Basilica holds regular services and special masses on feast days. Major religious observances include:

  • Daily Mass:
    • Held in English and Konkani, offering prayers, scripture readings, and the Eucharist.
  • Novena of Grace:
    • Held annually leading up to the feast of St. Francis Xavier, attracting large congregations.
  • Feast Day Celebrations:
    • On December 3rd, a grand mass and cultural events are held, honoring the saint's legacy.
  • Rosary and Adoration:
    • Conducted weekly, offering a time of reflection and connection with Christ through Mary.

Festivals

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is the heart of many Christian festivities in Goa. Notable ones include:

  • Feast of St. Francis Xavier: Celebrated on December 3rd with solemn masses, processions, and community feasts.
  • Christmas and Easter: Marked by special midnight masses, choir singing, and community events.
  • Good Friday: Includes the Stations of the Cross, reflecting on the Passion of Christ.
  • Exposition of the Relics: Held once every 10 years, the saint’s relics are displayed for public veneration over a month-long period.
