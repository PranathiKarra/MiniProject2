Tips and Rules for Pilgrimage to Sabarimala Temple

  1. The pilgrimage to Sabarimala traditionally begins with a 41-day *Vratham* (austerity period). Devotees should observe celibacy, practice vegetarianism, avoid alcohol, and follow a simple lifestyle during this period.

  2. Wear simple black, blue, or saffron-colored clothes during the pilgrimage, symbolizing renunciation. Avoid wearing footwear while climbing the hill.

  3. Only men and women above 10 and below 50 years (non-menstruating age) are traditionally allowed to visit the Sabarimala temple.

  4. Devotees usually carry the *Irumudi Kettu*—a sacred offering bundle on the head. Entry to the temple sanctum is allowed only with this ritual bundle.

  5. Plan your journey ahead. Book virtual queue (Darshan) slots through the official Sabarimala website or mobile app to avoid overcrowding.

  6. Start the trek from Pamba with a clean body and a peaceful mind. The path is sacred, and the climb should be accompanied by devotional chants like “Swamiye Saranam Ayyappa.”

  7. Photography is strictly prohibited near the sanctum sanctorum. Please respect the sanctity and refrain from taking pictures in sacred areas.

  8. Mobile phones must be kept on silent or switched off. Loud conversations or music are not permitted within temple premises.

  9. Do not litter. Maintain cleanliness along the trekking path and inside the temple. Use designated dustbins for waste disposal.

  10. Follow the instructions of the temple staff and police personnel. Queue discipline is essential to ensure everyone’s safe Darshan.

  11. After Darshan, Prasadam like *Aravana Payasam* and *Appam* can be purchased at counters. These are highly sought-after offerings of Lord Ayyappa.

  12. Be aware of your surroundings—especially near riverbanks and steep paths. Monkeys are common, so avoid displaying food openly.
