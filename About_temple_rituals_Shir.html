Poojas or Rituals of the Temple

The daily ritual of worship at Shirdi Sai Baba Temple includes multiple Aartis, Poojas, and devotional activities. Each Aarti involves devotional singing, offerings (Naivedyam), and lighting of lamps (Deepa Aradanai) before Baba's Samadhi and idol.

  • Kakad Aarti (Morning Aarti):
    • This is the first Aarti of the day and begins at 4:30 AM. Devotees gather in large numbers to sing early morning bhajans and offer prayers. Baba's idol is then given a holy bath and decorated.

  • Mangal Snan (Holy Bath) and Alankaram:
    • Takes place after Kakad Aarti around 5:00 AM to 5:35 AM. Baba's idol is bathed, adorned with fresh clothes, garlands, and ornaments.

  • Mid-Morning Darshan and Pooja:
    • Devotees are allowed for darshan and offer prayers after decoration, from 5:40 AM onwards.

  • Midday Aarti (Madhyan Aarti):
    • This Aarti takes place at 12:00 PM. Aarti songs are sung in praise of Sai Baba, and Naivedyam is offered during this time.

  • Dhoop Aarti (Evening Aarti):
    • Held at sunset, typically around 6:30 PM to 7:00 PM. Lamps are lit, and evening bhajans are performed in the temple.

  • Palki Procession (Thursday Special):
    • Every Thursday at 9:15 PM, a special Palki procession takes place, where Sai Baba's idol is taken in a palanquin around the temple premises from Samadhi Mandir to Dwarkamai and Chavadi.

  • Shej Aarti (Night Aarti):
    • The final ritual of the day begins at 10:30 PM. Baba is symbolically put to rest with lullaby bhajans, offerings, and final Aarti.

Festivals

The temple celebrates three main annual festivals, each lasting around 2–4 days, with processions, adorations, music, and scripture recitations:


  • Ram Navami marks the birth of Lord Rama and is also celebrated as a significant festival in Shirdi, symbolizing the divine arrival of Sai Baba in Shirdi and his spiritual union with Lord Rama:
    • Day 1: Flag hoisting and night palanquin (Palkhi) procession of Sai Baba
    • Day 2: Grand chariot procession (Rathotsavam) with devotional singing and village tour
    • Day 3: Special poojas, reading of holy texts, and mass Annadanam for all devotees

  • The temple celebrates Guru Purnima with devotion to spiritual teachings and Guru worship:
    • Day 1: Evening palanquin procession from Samadhi Mandir through Dwarkamai and Chavadi
    • Day 2: Chariot procession, Guru Pooja, and spiritual discourses by saints and scholars
    • Day 3: Final Aarti, prasadam distribution, and bhajans in Samadhi Mandir

  • Vijayadashami marks the Mahasamadhi (Punyatithi) of Sai Baba and is one of the most sacred events at Shirdi:
    • Day 1: Evening palanquin procession commemorating Baba’s Samadhi
    • Day 2: Chariot procession (Rathotsavam), rituals marking Baba’s remembrance day
    • Day 3: Bhajans, reading of Sai Satcharitra, final Aarti, and mass feeding (Annadanam)
