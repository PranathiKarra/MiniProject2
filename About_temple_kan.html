Kanchi Kamakshi Amman Temple

About Kamakshi Amman Temple

Arulmigu Kamakshi Amman Temple, also known as Sri Kamakshi Amman Thirukkovil, is a revered Hindu temple located in the ancient city of Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, India. Dedicated to Goddess Kamakshi, an incarnation of Parvati (Shakti), this temple holds immense spiritual significance and is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas in India. The temple stands at the heart of Kanchipuram, a city celebrated as one of the seven Moksha-puris (sacred cities for liberation) in Hinduism. Believed to have been established during the Pallava dynasty, the Kamakshi Temple is the only shrine in Kanchipuram exclusively dedicated to Shakti, while the rest are predominantly Shiva or Vishnu temples.

The temple is a fine example of classical South Indian architecture, with intricate sculptures, a majestic sanctum, and sacred mandapams. It has deep connections to Adi Shankaracharya, who is believed to have established the Sri Chakra here to pacify the fierce form of the Goddess. Kamakshi Amman Temple is a major pilgrimage destination and draws thousands of devotees daily. During the Navaratri Festival, the temple is visited by lakhs of pilgrims who come to witness the grand celebrations and spiritual fervor.

Architecture Of the Temple

Here are some important details regarding the architecture of Kamakshi temple:

  1. The temple showcases classic Dravidian architecture, with a majestic Raja Gopuram, beautifully carved mandapams, and a serene sanctum where Goddess Kamakshi is uniquely seated in Padmasana posture.
  2. Inside the temple complex are sacred spaces like the Pushkarini (temple tank), the Gayatri Mandapam, and shrines dedicated to deities including Vinayaka, Vishnu, and Adi Shankaracharya.
  3. It is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas and the only major temple in Kanchipuram dedicated solely to Shakti, enhancing its spiritual and mythological importance.
  4. The astonishing multi-layer architecture of the temple covers over 5 acres of land. Mark with stunning gopuram at the entrance and the gopuram is a tower-like structure that is furnished with statues of God and Goddesses.
  5. One can enter the temple from the four entrances of the temple, inside the holy land the Goddess can be seen sitting in Padmasana. She held the bow in their upper left arm, a parrot in her upper right arm, along divine chakras of Pasa and Angular in her lower arms.

Facts about Kamakshi Temple

People from all over the world flock to see the temple because it is so majestic and grand. Here are some more fascinating facts about the temple that you should be aware of. So look at it.

  1. Only Shakti Temple in Kanchipuram
    • Despite being a city of thousands of temples, Kamakshi Amman Temple is the only major temple in Kanchipuram dedicated to Goddess Shakti (Parvati).

  2. One of the 51 Shakti Peethas
    • It is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, sacred sites where parts of Sati Devi’s body are believed to have fallen.

  3. Established by Pallavas
    • The temple dates back to the Pallava dynasty, around the 6th century CE, and was later expanded by the Cholas and Vijayanagara kings.

About Kamakshi Amman

    Kamakshi (Sanskrit: कामाक्षी, lit. Kāmākṣī, Tamil: காமாட்சி, lit. Kāmāṭci) means “She whose eyes awaken desire/love” or “the one with loving or graceful eyes.” The name is derived from “Kama” (desire or love) and “Akshi” (eyes), signifying her compassionate gaze that grants boons and blessings to devotees.She is also known as “Shanta Swarupini”the embodiment of peace—and lovingly referred to in Tamil as Kanchipurathu Kamakshi.

    According to legend, Kamakshi is a divine incarnation of Parvati, the supreme goddess and consort of Lord Shiva. In a previous cosmic age, when the world was filled with chaos, the goddess took a fierce form to destroy evil. Her anger and energy were so intense that even the gods were frightened. To bring peace to the universe, Adi Shankaracharya is said to have established a Sri Chakra in the sanctum of the Kamakshi temple, transforming her from a fierce deity into her present benevolent, peaceful form.

    Another version of the legend narrates that Goddess Kamakshi performed penance under a sacred mango tree on the banks of the temple tank (Kamalalayam) in Kanchipuram, using a Shiva Lingam made of sand. To test her devotion, Lord Shiva sent floodwaters and fire, but the goddess protected the lingam with her body and her love. Pleased with her devotion, Lord Shiva appeared before her and agreed to marry her as Ekambaranatha, a union that is spiritually commemorated in both the Kamakshi and Ekambareswarar temples of Kanchipuram.Though Shiva temples are numerous in Kanchipuram, Kamakshi Amman is the only principal Shakti temple, symbolizing the central energy (Shakti) of the city. She is always depicted in a Padmasana (lotus) posture, a rare and deeply meditative stance, holding sugarcane, flower arrows, a noose, and a goad—symbolizing love, control over the senses, and spiritual awakening.
