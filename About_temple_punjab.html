Golden Temple Temple

About Golden Temple

Sri Harmandir Sahib, also known as the Golden Temple, is a prominent Sikh Gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the holiest shrine in Sikhism and is open to people of all faiths. The temple was founded by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru, in 1577 and later completed by Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Sikh Guru, in 1604. The sanctum was overlaid with gold foil during the early 19th century under the patronage of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, giving it the iconic golden appearance. Surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar (Pool of Nectar), the temple represents infinite peace, equality, and spirituality.

The Golden Temple is not just a religious site but a symbol of human brotherhood and equality. It was built as a place of worship for all, regardless of caste, creed, or race. The four entrances to the temple from all directions signify the openness of the Sikhs towards all people and religions. The Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism, is recited continuously at the temple. The temple complex also houses the Akal Takht (Throne of the Timeless One), the highest seat of earthly authority of the Khalsa. The Golden Temple is visited by millions of devotees every year and is a beacon of peace and harmony.

Architecture of the Temple

Here are some important details regarding the architecture of the Golden Temple:

  1. The temple is a blend of Indo-Islamic Mughal and traditional Sikh architectural styles, symbolizing a unique spiritual experience.
  2. The sanctum is built on a lower level than the surrounding land to symbolize humility, and it is surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar, a man-made pool of sacred water.
  3. The temple's upper floors are covered in approximately 750 kg of pure gold, making it one of the most stunning religious sites in the world.
  4. A causeway connects the Harmandir Sahib to the main entrance gate, known as Darshani Deori.
  5. The central dome represents an inverted lotus, symbolizing detachment from the material world.
  6. The temple complex includes the Akal Takht, various prayer halls, libraries, and the world's largest free community kitchen (Langar) serving up to 100,000 meals daily.

Facts about the Golden Temple

People from all walks of life and from across the world visit the Golden Temple to seek peace and offer prayers. Here are some fascinating facts about the temple you should know:

  1. The Golden Temple receives over 6 million visitors every year, making it one of the most visited religious places in the world.
  2. It serves free food (Langar) to tens of thousands of people daily, regardless of religion or background, through a well-organized volunteer system.
  3. The foundation of the Golden Temple was laid by a Muslim saint, Hazrat Mian Mir, in a gesture of unity and brotherhood.
  4. The temple has entrances on all four sides, symbolizing openness to all people from all directions and walks of life.
  5. The gold plating was carried out under the reign of Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the early 1800s.
  6. The Guru Granth Sahib is recited 24/7, and the holy book is ceremonially taken to and from the Akal Takht daily.
  7. The temple remains open throughout the year and glows beautifully when lit up during festivals like Vaisakhi and Gurpurab.

About the Golden Temple

    The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib (literally, "The Abode of God"), is the most revered spiritual site of Sikhism. The word "Harmandir" is derived from the words "Hari" (God) and "Mandir" (temple), meaning the Temple of God. It symbolizes openness, equality, and humility. The temple was designed with four entrances, one on each side, to signify that it welcomes people from all walks of life and religions.

    The foundation stone of the temple was laid in 1581 by the Muslim saint Hazrat Mian Mir on the invitation of Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the fifth Sikh Guru. This powerful act symbolized interfaith harmony. The construction of the temple was completed in 1604, and the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of the Sikhs, was installed inside.

    The Golden Temple is built in the middle of the Amrit Sarovar (Pool of Nectar), from which the city of Amritsar gets its name. Devotees bathe in this holy water, which is believed to purify the soul. The entire structure is connected to the land by a marble causeway that represents the spiritual journey toward enlightenment.

    Unlike Hindu temples where deities are worshipped in idol form, the Golden Temple is centered around the Guru Granth Sahib — the eternal Guru of the Sikhs. The scripture is recited continuously and carried ceremonially each morning and night to and from the Akal Takht, a sacred seat of Sikh authority located within the complex.

    Over time, the upper part of the temple was covered with nearly 750 kg of pure gold, added during the reign of Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the early 19th century. This iconic gilded dome gave the temple its popular name — "The Golden Temple." Today, it stands as a universal symbol of peace, community service, and spiritual reflection.
