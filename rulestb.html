Tips and Rules for visiting the Temple

  1. Don’t wear revealing clothes that expose too much skin. Wear clothes that cover your legs and arms completely. Advisable for women to wear shawls. The temple authorities are very strict about the dress code so do make sure you are conservatively dressed. Devotees must wear traditional Indian attire—men in dhoti and shirt or angavastram, and women in saree, salwar kameez, or half-saree; western clothing like jeans and sleeveless tops is not permitted.

  6. Use the official TTD website or app to book Special Entry (₹300), Free Darshan, or Sevas, as tickets fill fast during festivals and weekends.

  7. Reserve TTD guesthouses or private hotels in Tirumala or Tirupati, with TTD offering clean, budget-friendly rooms and basic amenities.

  8. Carry your ticket and ID, be ready for security checks and long waits, and embrace the few sacred seconds of Lord Venkateswara's darshan with devotion.

  9. Hair Offering (Kalyanakatta); As a mark of devotion, many pilgrims shave their heads—ensure cleanliness and wait your turn respectfully.

  10. Visitors are encouraged to avoid plastic use to help preserve Tirumala’s natural beauty and support eco-friendly practices.

  11. Maintain a peaceful and respectful atmosphere by observing silence, especially within the sanctum and other sacred areas of the temple.

  12. Keep yourself refreshed and energized during the visit by staying well-hydrated, especially in the Tirumala hill’s warm and crowded conditions.
