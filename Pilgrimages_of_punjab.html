Pilgrimages of Punjab

Tamil Nadu has a lot of religious sites, but the most famous and renowned pilgrimage sites are:

Amritsar

Amritsar, located in the northwestern state of Punjab, is home to the world-famous Golden Temple (Sri Harmandir Sahib). This sacred Sikh shrine, surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar, is a symbol of peace, service, and spirituality, drawing millions of pilgrims and visitors every year.

Kancheepuram

Kanchipuram or Kanchi is one of the most famous places of pilgrimage in Tamil Nadu. It is situated at a distance of 75 km from Chennai. Located on the bank of the Palar River, Kanchipuram is one of the oldest cities in India and served as the capital of the Pallava Dynasty.

Rameswaram

Rameswaram, an island in southeastern Tamil Nadu, contains one of the most venerated Hindu shrines. Together with Varanasi, it is considered one of the holiest places in India and part of the Char Dham pilgrimage.

Tiruvannamalai

The sacred site of Tiruvannamalai is one of the prominent pilgrimage centers in Tamil Nadu. Located about 196 km from Chennai, it is known for the Annamalaiyar Temple and its spiritual significance in Shaivism.

Kanyakumari

Located at the southernmost tip of the Indian Peninsula, Kanyakumari is one of the famous pilgrimage places to visit as part of a Tamil Nadu tour. It is about 242 km from Madurai and 707 km from Chennai.
