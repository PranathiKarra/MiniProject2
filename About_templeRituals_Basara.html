Poojas or Rituals of the Temple

The Basara Temple follows a daily routine of Poojas and rituals performed with devotion to Goddess Gnana Saraswati. Key rituals include:

  • Suprabhata Seva:
    • Early morning prayer to awaken the deity, generally starts at 4:00 AM.
  • Abhishekam:
    • The idol is bathed with sacred substances like milk, honey, curd, ghee, etc.
  • Alankarana:
    • The deity is decorated beautifully with ornaments and flowers.
  • Archana and Aarti:
    • Special prayers and offering of light are performed during morning and evening hours.
  • Aksharabhyasam:
    • This is the most famous ritual in Basara, where children are initiated into learning by writing their first letters in rice grains in the presence of the goddess.
  • Annadanam:
    • Daily free food service is offered to devotees, especially during special occasions and festivals.

Festivals

Several festivals are celebrated at Basara Temple with great fervor. The major festivals include:

  • Vasant Panchami: Dedicated to Goddess Saraswati, marking the arrival of spring. Special Aksharabhyasam ceremonies are conducted.
  • Navaratri: Nine-day festival celebrated with cultural and spiritual activities. Daily Poojas and Aartis are performed.
  • Dasara: Grand celebrations with special rituals, especially on Saraswati Puja day.
  • Guru Purnima: Special prayers and gatherings to honor gurus (teachers and spiritual guides).
  • Kartika Purnima: Special evening Aarti and lamp lighting ceremony on the riverbank.
