Poojas or Rituals of the Temple

  • Kakad Aarti (Morning Aarti):
    • Around 5:30 AM ;the temple opens with Kakad Aarti. Devotees are allowed to witness the Lord being awakened with the sound of bells and conches. The idol is bathed and decorated.

  • Abhishekam (Sacred Bath Ritual):
    • The Abhishekam begins soon after 6:00 AM; the main Lingam is bathed with sacred water, milk, honey, and other offerings. The Abhishekam is done in multiple rounds. Devotees can participate by purchasing tickets.

  • Rudrabhishek:
    • Throughout the morning till noon special Vedic chanting of Rudra Sukta is performed during this ritual, which is considered highly auspicious. Can be done individually or as a group.

  • Mahapooja:
    • Around 11:30 AM ;a grand pooja with full offerings including Naivedyam, Deepa Aarti, and mantras, done by the temple priests.

  • Madhyahna Aarti (Midday Aarti):
    • Around 12:00 Noon; a short worship ritual with Aarti, marking the midday closure.And temple remains closed from 1:00 PM to 4:00 PM.

  • Sayankal Aarti (Evening Aarti):
    • A round 4:30 PM – 5:30 PM after reopening, another Aarti is performed in the evening, accompanied by chants and lamps..

  • Shej Aarti (Night Aarti):
    • 8:00 PM – 8:30 PM;The final pooja of the day, where the Lord is ceremoniously put to rest. The temple closes shortly after.

Festivals

The most important festival here is Maha Shivaratri, celebrated grandly during February–March, but Kumbh Mela (every 12 years) and Shravan month (July–August) are also major events.

  • Day 1:Flag hoisting (Dwajarohanam) – Official start of the Maha Shivaratri Utsav marked by decorating the Dwajastambha and performing opening rituals.
  • Day 2–6: Rudrabhishekams and Vedic chanting – Daily multiple Rudrabhishekams with Vedic hymns and large devotee participation.
  • Day 7: Procession of Nandi (Vrushabha Vahanam) – Evening procession of Lord Shiva on Nandi vahanam through temple streets.
  • Day 8: Day 8: Maha Shivaratri night rituals All night temple opening with four kala poojas, Abhishekams, and continuous chanting.
  • Day 9: Ratha Yatra – Grand chariot procession of the deity with bhajans and large public gathering.
  • Day 10: Kalyanotsavam-Symbolic divine wedding of Lord Trimbakeshwar and Goddess Parvati.
  • Day 11:Teerth Snan (Holy Dip in Kushavarta Kund)-Devotees take sacred dip in Kushavarta Teerth for spiritual purification.
  • Day 12:Deepotsav and conclusion-Temple illuminated with lamps followed by final aarti and ritual closure of the festival.
