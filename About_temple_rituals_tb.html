Poojas or Rituals of the Temple

At the Tirumala temple, worship is performed eight times a day, following the Vaikhanasa Agama tradition, which includes the core elements you mentioned: Abhishekam, Alankaram, Naivedyam, and Deepa Aradhana. Here's how it's performed:

  • Suprabhata Seva :
    • Suprabhata Seva at 3:00 AM marks the ceremonial waking of Lord Venkateswara with sacred Suprabhatam hymns.

  • Tomala Seva (Flower Decoration Service):
    • Tomala Seva is an early morning ritual where Lord Venkateswara is adorned with fresh flower garlands.

  • Koluvu Seva (Royal Court Ritual):
    • Temple priests read out the daily income and expenses, as Lord is treated like a king.

  • Archana Seva (Sahasranamarchana):
    • reciting 1,000 names of Lord Vishnu, offering tulasi leaves.

  • Kalyanotsavam (Symbolic Wedding Seva):
    • Performed as a Utsavam (ceremony), symbolic marriage with consorts.

  • Arjitha Brahmotsavam (Afternoon seva):
    • Deity is placed on a swing (Unjal) and hymns are sung.

  • Sayana Seva (Evening Rest Ritual):
    • Deity is prepared for rest, performed with lullabies and soft lighting.

  • Ekanta Seva (Final Ritual of the Day):
      Ekanta Seva, performed around 9:00 PM, is the final ritual where Lord Venkateswara is laid to rest with offerings of milk, fruits, and water.

    • Festivals

    There are festivals celebrated at the temple every month; however, the Brahmotsavam in September–October is the most important one. This grand annual festival is believed to be initiated by Lord Brahma to honor Lord Venkateswara. Other aspects of the festival are as follows:

    • Day 1: Flag hoisting (Dwajarohanam), marking the official start of the festival
    • Day 2: Procession of the deity on Chinna Sesha Vahanam (morning) and Hamsa Vahanam (evening)
    • Day 3: Simha Vahanam (lion vehicle) and Muthyapu Pandiri Vahanam (pearl canopy)
    • Day 4: Kalpavriksha Vahanam and Sarvabhoopala Vahanam (symbolizing universal sovereignty)
    • Day 5: Garuda Vahanam — the most significant procession of the festival
    • Day 6: Hanumantha Vahanam and Gaja Vahanam (elephant)
    • Day 7: Surya Prabha Vahanam (sun) and Chandra Prabha Vahanam (moon)
    • Day 8: Rathotsavam (grand chariot procession)
    • Day 9: Chakra Snanam (holy bath of the Sudarshana Chakra) and concluding rituals
