Tips and Rules for visiting the Temple

    1. Plan your visit early in the morning or late evening for a peaceful experience and to witness the beautiful Palki Sahib ceremony (scripture procession).

    2. Dress modestly and respectfully. Both men and women must cover their heads with a scarf or handkerchief before entering the temple premises.

    3. Footwear must be removed before entering the complex. Wash your hands and feet at the designated area before entering.

    4. Silence and discipline should be maintained inside the temple premises. Avoid loud conversations or rushing inside the sanctum.

    5. Mobile phones should be kept on silent mode. Photography is allowed in the outer complex, but not inside the sanctum.

    6. Langar (free community meal) is served to all visitors regardless of religion or background. Sit in rows and follow the volunteers' instructions respectfully.

    7. Do not consume alcohol, tobacco, or any intoxicants before visiting. Entry is not allowed under the influence of such substances.

    8. Be respectful to all volunteers (Sevadars) and staff. They serve selflessly and help maintain the sacredness of the temple.

    9. Do not litter in or around the temple. Use the dustbins provided. Cleanliness is a vital part of Sikh values.

    10. If traveling with children or elderly, plan extra time for rest or sitting in the prayer hall to soak in the spiritual ambiance.

    11. Be cautious around the Amrit Sarovar (holy pool). Though bathing is allowed in designated areas, avoid crowding or pushing.

    12. Carry only necessary belongings; large bags may be checked or restricted at the entrance.
