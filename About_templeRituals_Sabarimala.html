Poojas or Rituals of the Temple

The Sabarimala Temple observes a set of daily and seasonal rituals devoted to Lord Ayyappa, reflecting deep spiritual discipline. Major poojas and practices include:

  • Suprabhatham:
    • Early morning hymns and prayers are offered to awaken Lord Ayyappa, generally around 3:00 AM to 3:30 AM.
  • Ganapati Homam:
    • A fire ritual conducted early in the morning to invoke Lord Ganesha’s blessings before starting other poojas.
  • Usha Pooja:
    • Morning worship performed after the opening of the sanctum, includes offerings and chanting of mantras.
  • Ucha Pooja:
    • Midday ritual involving offerings of cooked rice, sweets, and sacred chants to Lord Ayyappa.
  • Deeparadhana:
    • Evening worship with oil lamps and devotional songs, creating a divine and serene atmosphere in the temple premises.
  • Pushpabhishekam:
    • Ritual bath of the deity using fresh flowers, typically performed on auspicious days or special occasions.
  • Harivarasanam:
    • A devotional lullaby sung to Lord Ayyappa before closing the sanctum at night. It’s a moving experience for all devotees.

Festivals

The Sabarimala Temple celebrates several festivals that attract millions of devotees. Prominent ones include:

  • Mandala Pooja: A 41-day vratham (austerity period) culminating in a special pooja. Marks one of the main pilgrimage seasons (Nov–Dec).
  • Makaravilakku: Celebrated in January, this festival involves the sighting of the celestial light (Makara Jyothi) on Ponnambalamedu hill, believed to be divine.
  • Vishu: Kerala New Year celebrated in April. Special offerings and Vishu Kani are arranged for Lord Ayyappa.
  • Onam: While not a primary temple festival, Onam is observed with reverence, with offerings and prayers by local devotees.
  • Thulam Month Opening: The temple reopens in the Malayalam month of Thulam (Oct–Nov), considered auspicious by Ayyappa devotees.
