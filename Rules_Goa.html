Tips and Rules for Visiting the Basilica of Bom Jesus

  1. The Basilica is a revered Christian pilgrimage site. Visitors are encouraged to maintain silence and a respectful demeanor within the church premises.

  2. Dress modestly—covering shoulders and knees—as a sign of respect to the sanctity of the church. Avoid flashy or revealing clothing.

  3. The church is open to people of all faiths. However, religious rituals or practices unrelated to Christianity should be avoided inside the basilica.

  4. Visitors can attend Mass and prayer services, especially on Sundays and feast days. Seating arrangements and liturgical customs should be followed reverently.

  5. Photography is permitted inside the church but should be done without using flash or disrupting prayer services. Avoid photographing worshippers during mass.

  6. Maintain the sanctity of the mausoleum of St. Francis Xavier. Do not touch or lean on the relics or display any casual behavior near the tomb.

  7. Large crowds gather during the feast of St. Francis Xavier (December 3rd). Plan your visit accordingly and follow local instructions during processions or special events.

  8. Do not litter within the church complex. Use dustbins and help keep this World Heritage Site clean and dignified.

  9. Mobile phones should be on silent mode. Loud conversations, music, or video calls are discouraged within sacred spaces.

  10. Food, drinks, and chewing gum are not allowed inside the basilica. Consume refreshments only in designated areas outside the main hall.

  11. Guided tours are available—use licensed guides or audio guides to learn about the rich history and architecture of the basilica.

  12. Respect the clergy and staff, and follow their instructions—especially during peak hours and religious ceremonies.
