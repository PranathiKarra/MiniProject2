About Old Goa City

Old Goa, also known as Velha Goa, is a historic city situated in the North Goa district of the Indian state of Goa. Once the flourishing capital of Portuguese India, the city is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site famed for its magnificent churches and cathedrals. Among its many iconic structures, the Basilica of Bom Jesus stands as a spiritual and architectural marvel.

Surrounded by lush greenery and the calm banks of the Mandovi River, Old Goa offers a striking contrast between colonial grandeur and natural serenity. Though not a bustling city today, it transforms into a center of spiritual and cultural activity, especially during festive seasons and annual religious feasts.

The nearest urban center is Panjim (Panaji), located approximately 10 km away, which serves as a convenient base for tourists and pilgrims. Other important nearby towns include Vasco da Gama and Margao, both well-connected through road and rail.

Even as a historical site, Old Goa remains well-maintained with efforts from the Archaeological Survey of India and the Church authorities. It continues to attract both devotees and tourists from across the world, showcasing a harmonious blend of history, faith, and heritage in one of India’s most celebrated Christian pilgrimage destinations.

History of Basilica of Bom Jesus

The Basilica of Bom Jesus, built in 1594 and consecrated in 1605, is one of the oldest and most revered churches in India. It is especially significant in Christian history as it houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, a Spanish missionary who played a crucial role in spreading Christianity in Asia during the 16th century.

Constructed in Baroque architectural style, the basilica stands as a testament to Portuguese craftsmanship and religious dedication. The name “Bom Jesus” means “Good Jesus” or “Infant Jesus,” and the church is dedicated to the Holy Name of Jesus. Over the centuries, it has remained a symbol of faith and a prominent pilgrimage site for Catholics.

Every ten years, the church hosts the public viewing of St. Francis Xavier’s body, drawing thousands of pilgrims from around the world. The church’s timeless aura, exquisite interiors, and sacred relics make it a must-visit for anyone seeking to experience the spiritual and historical richness of Goa.
