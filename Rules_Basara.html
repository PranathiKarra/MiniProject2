Tips and Rules for Aksharabhyasam at Basara Temple

  1. Aksharabhyasam is usually performed for children aged 2 to 5 years. It is recommended to choose an auspicious day such as Vasant Panchami, Sravana Pournami, or Navratri.

  2. Arrive early in the morning to avoid crowds, especially on weekends and festival days.

  3. Dress modestly and traditionally. Children should preferably wear ethnic attire like dhoti or frock, and parents should wear Indian traditional clothes.

  4. Carry essential items like slate, chalk or rice plate (Akshata), coconut, flowers, fruits, and turmeric-kumkum as they are needed during the ritual.

  5. There is an Aksharabhyasam counter inside the temple premises where you can buy a ticket for the ritual. The staff will guide you to the designated priest.

  6. The fee for Aksharabhyasam typically ranges between ₹100 and ₹300, depending on the package you choose.

  7. Photography may be restricted inside the sanctum; kindly respect temple rules regarding photos and videos.

  8. Keep mobile phones on silent mode or switched off inside the temple premises.

  9. Maintain silence and decorum inside the temple. Follow the instructions of the priests and temple authorities at all times.

  10. After the ritual, prasadam will be offered to the child and family. You can also purchase extra prasadam from temple counters.

  11. Wash hands and feet before entering the sanctum area.

  12. Be cautious of monkeys around the temple and avoid carrying food items openly.
