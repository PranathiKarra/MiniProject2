Rituals and Ceremonies at the Basilica of Bom Jesus

The Basilica of Bom Jesus follows Roman Catholic traditions and conducts regular liturgical services honoring Jesus Christ and St. Francis Xavier. Key rituals and spiritual practices include:

  • Daily Mass:
    • Conducted in Konkani, English, and Latin. These services include Bible readings, homily, and Holy Communion.
  • Adoration of the Blessed Sacrament:
    • A time of silent prayer and meditation in the presence of the Eucharist, often held on Fridays or feast days.
  • Novena to St. Francis Xavier:
    • Special prayers and Masses offered for nine days prior to the Feast of St. Francis Xavier (Nov 24 – Dec 2).
  • Rosary and Benediction:
    • Devotees gather in the evenings to pray the Rosary followed by Benediction of the Blessed Sacrament.
  • Sacrament of Reconciliation:
    • Confession is available for pilgrims seeking spiritual renewal and forgiveness.

Festivals

The Basilica hosts grand celebrations that attract thousands of pilgrims and tourists from across the globe. Notable events include:

  • Feast of St. Francis Xavier (Dec 3): The most significant event, featuring a High Mass, novena prayers, a grand procession, and veneration of the saint’s relics.
  • Christmas: Celebrated with Midnight Mass, carols, nativity scenes, and festive decorations throughout the church.
  • Good Friday & Easter: Solemn liturgies, Stations of the Cross, and Easter Vigil Mass mark the Holy Week observances.
  • Feast of the Immaculate Conception (Dec 8): A revered Marian feast celebrated with special Masses and processions.
  • Lenten Retreats: Conducted during Lent, these include confessions, teachings, and spiritual exercises for the faithful.
